03

Zero-Knowledge Architecture

This section provides a technical explanation of how Anchor Cloud's zero-knowledge design protects your file privacy. We believe you have a right to understand exactly how your data is protected.

When you upload a file:

  1. Your file is read as raw bytes by the Anchor Cloud backend server
  2. A unique 256-bit AES encryption key is derived from your User ID and a server-side Master Salt using PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 with 200,000 iterations — this key is never stored anywhere
  3. The file is encrypted using AES-256-EAX authenticated encryption with a randomly generated 16-byte nonce
  4. The encrypted blob (nonce + MAC tag + ciphertext) is written to disk — only the encrypted form is ever persisted
  5. File metadata (name, size, type, UUID) is stored in our MySQL database — but not the file content

What this means in practice: Even if our entire database and file storage were stolen in a breach, an attacker would obtain only encrypted blobs that are computationally infeasible to decrypt without the Master Salt, which is stored only in our server environment and never in the database.

When you download a file, the decryption key is re-derived on-the-fly using your authenticated session, the file is decrypted in memory, and the plaintext is streamed directly to you. No plaintext version of your file is ever written to disk.

Data Sharing & Disclosure

Anchor Cloud does not sell, trade, rent, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties for commercial purposes. We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We may share data with trusted third-party vendors who assist in operating the Service (e.g., cloud infrastructure, email delivery), subject to strict confidentiality agreements that prohibit them from using your data for any other purpose
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, court order, subpoena, or other legal process, or if we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, protect your safety or the safety of others, or investigate fraud
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or a portion of our assets, your personal information may be transferred as part of the transaction. We will notify you via email and/or prominent notice on our website before your data is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy
  • With Your Consent: We may share your information with third parties when you have given us explicit consent to do so

We note that due to our zero-knowledge architecture, even in cases of legally compelled disclosure, we can only provide file metadata — we are technically incapable of providing the contents of your encrypted files.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.

Data Type Retention Period
Account data Until account deletion request, then 30 days for processing
Encrypted file blobs Until file deletion by user, then immediately purged from disk
File metadata Until file or account deletion
Server logs (IP, access) 90 days, then automatically purged
Billing records 7 years (legal/tax requirements)

When you delete a file, the encrypted blob is immediately removed from disk storage. When you delete your account, all associated files, metadata, and personal data are purged within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Anchor Cloud uses a minimal set of cookies and similar technologies necessary to operate the Service. We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or third-party analytics that profile your behavior across websites.

  • Session Cookies: Used to maintain your authenticated session while you are logged in. These are essential for the Service to function and cannot be disabled

You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences. Disabling essential cookies may prevent the Service from functioning correctly.

International Data Transfers

Anchor Cloud operates globally, and your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have different data protection laws than your country.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have additional rights under the GDPR and equivalent legislation. Please contact us at contact@anchorcloud.org to exercise these rights.

Security Measures

Anchor Cloud implements comprehensive technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction:

  • Encryption at Rest: All file contents are encrypted using AES-256-EAX before being written to disk
  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Password Security: Passwords are hashed using bcrypt with a minimum cost factor of 12 — plain-text passwords are never stored
  • Key Derivation: AES keys are derived using PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 with 200,000 iterations — never stored in any form
  • JWT Security: Session tokens are signed with HS256 and expire after 24 hours
  • Access Controls: Strict role-based access controls ensure employees can only access systems necessary for their job functions
  • Security Audits: We conduct periodic security reviews and welcome responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities

In the event of a data breach that affects your personal data, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by applicable law, typically within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.
